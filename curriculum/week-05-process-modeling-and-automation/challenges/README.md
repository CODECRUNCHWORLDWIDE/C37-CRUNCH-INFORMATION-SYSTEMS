# Week 5 — Challenges

Two challenges. Harder than the exercises, less scaffolded, and closer to what "process automation" work actually feels like on the job — one open-ended, one a debugging exercise with a real bug planted on purpose.

| # | Challenge | Difficulty | Time |
|---|-----------|------------|-----:|
| 1 | [challenge-01-automate-a-real-process.md](./challenge-01-automate-a-real-process.md) | Medium — open-ended, no answer key | 90 min |
| 2 | [challenge-02-make-it-idempotent.md](./challenge-02-make-it-idempotent.md) | Medium-Hard — diagnose a real class of bug | 60 min |

## How to work them

- Challenge 1 leaves the CrunchRide scenario — you pick a real manual process from your own life, job, or school. There's no seed data provided; part of the challenge is describing your own process precisely enough that someone else could model it from your write-up alone.
- Challenge 2 comes with a script that runs fine once and breaks the second time — your job is to find every way it breaks, not just patch the first bug you spot.
- Neither has a single "correct" answer key. Both are judged by a rubric, same as this week's lecture material — read the rubric in each file before you start, not after.

## Submission

Same convention as the exercises: commit each challenge's deliverable to `c37-week-05/challenge-0N/` in your portfolio.
