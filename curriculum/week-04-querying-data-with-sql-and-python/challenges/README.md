# Week 4 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems. Unlike the exercises, these don't hand you a checklist of expected numbers for every step — you're expected to reason about trade-offs and document your own decisions, the way a real analytics or platform engineer would.

## How to work these

1. Do both exercises and lectures first — these challenges assume you're fluent with joins, aggregation, `read_sql`/`to_sql`, and the ETL shape from Lecture 3.
2. Each challenge produces a short write-up alongside your code. The write-up is not optional — the *reasoning* is most of what's being graded.
3. There's no single correct answer to either challenge. A well-argued, clearly documented choice beats a "correct-looking" answer with no justification.
