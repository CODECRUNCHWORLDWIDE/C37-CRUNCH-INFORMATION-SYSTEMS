# Challenges — Week 3

Two challenges, each stretching a different muscle the exercises didn't fully exercise. Challenge 1 hands you a messy, real-world-shaped interview transcript instead of a clean brief — the ambiguity and noise are the point. Challenge 2 asks you to argue *against* the normalize-everything instinct the lectures spent two hours building, which is exactly the kind of judgment call real system designers make constantly.

## How to work them

- These are **harder and less scaffolded** than the exercises on purpose. There isn't a single right diagram or a single right justification — there's a defensible one and an indefensible one, and the difference is whether you can explain your reasoning.
- Time-box yourself. If you're stuck past the estimate, write down what's blocking you and move on — a partial answer with a clear statement of what's unresolved is worth more than silence.
- Both challenges assume you've done all three exercises first.
