# Week 12 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems, both built directly on your Exercise 1–3 work. There's no single right answer — the grade is in the *reasoning*, the trade-offs you name, and whether your design survives contact with a skeptical human or a changed constraint. Write each as a short design memo, as if you were sending it to a real stakeholder.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Defend under pressure](challenge-01-defend-under-pressure.md)** — a hostile review script pokes at your architecture's weakest points; answer every question using Lecture 3's four-part trade-off structure.
2. **[Challenge 2 — Re-architect on new constraints](challenge-02-re-architect-on-new-constraints.md)** — your budget is cut 60% or your user base grows 20x overnight; redesign the phasing, cost model, and architecture to survive it.

## How these are judged

- **Groundedness.** Every claim traces back to your actual Exercise 1–3 work, not invented-on-the-spot numbers.
- **Honesty about weaknesses.** A design with no acknowledged weak points reads as either untested or dishonest — name yours before the reviewer finds them.
- **Structural discipline.** Challenge 1 specifically checks whether you use Lecture 3's four-part answer (decision, rejected alternative, real trade-off, what would change your mind) — freeform defensiveness doesn't pass.
- **Adaptability, not restart.** Challenge 2 checks whether you can *reshape* an existing plan under new constraints, not throw it out and start over — real re-architecture reuses what still holds.

Write each as a 1–2 page memo. Save to `c37-week-12/challenge-01/` and `c37-week-12/challenge-02/`.
