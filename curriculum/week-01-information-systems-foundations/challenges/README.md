# Week 1 — Challenges

Two challenges, ~1 hour each. These are harder and more open-ended than the exercises — less "fill in the query," more "figure out what's actually wrong, then defend your fix." Do both after finishing all three exercises.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Diagnose the before-state](challenge-01-diagnose-the-before-state.md)** — find exactly what was missing in Riverbend's pre-register, paper-and-spreadsheet days, and fix it properly in SQL.
2. **[Challenge 2 — Model a new location](challenge-02-model-a-new-location.md)** — Riverbend opens a second café; decide what's shared across locations vs. what must be duplicated.

## Before you start

- All three exercises are done, including your extended register (components `21`–`25` and their flows).
- You're comfortable with the fast classification checklist from Lecture 2 §6 and the "look at the flow, not just the component" habit from Lecture 3 §4 — both challenges lean on them hard.

## How these are graded (self-check)

There's no single correct answer key for either challenge — that's deliberate; real systems analysis rarely has one. Instead, for each challenge ask yourself:

- Did I name the *specific* missing component or flow, not just "communication was bad"?
- Did I trace the consequence forward to an actual business impact, the way Lecture 3 §4 modeled?
- Is my fix expressed as real `INSERT` statements against the register, not just prose?
